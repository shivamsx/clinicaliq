# Appointment Guide -- Apollo Health Clinic

## How to Book an Appointment

Apollo Health Clinic offers three ways to book an appointment:

**Phone:** Call our reception at 080-33001100 (Mon-Sat 8am-8pm, Sunday 9am-1pm). Have the department or doctor name ready. Walk-in appointments are accepted subject to availability, but prior bookings are given priority.

**App:** Download the Apollo Health Clinic app (available on Android and iOS). Log in with your registered mobile number, select a department or doctor, choose a date and time slot, and confirm. You will receive a booking confirmation via SMS and email.

**Walk-in:** Visit the clinic reception counter. Walk-ins are accepted but wait times may be longer than for booked appointments, especially during peak morning hours (9am-11am). For specialist departments (Cardiology, Orthopaedics), prior booking is strongly recommended.

## Pre-Consultation Checklist

Bring the following to your appointment:

- **Government-issued photo ID** (Aadhaar card, PAN card, driver's licence, or passport) -- required for registration and insurance claims
- **Previous medical reports** relevant to your current concern -- lab reports, imaging (X-rays, MRI, ultrasound), ECG printouts, discharge summaries
- **List of current medications** -- include name, dose, and how often you take each. Include over-the-counter supplements and vitamins
- **Insurance card or TPA letter** if you intend to claim through health insurance
- **Previous Apollo Health Clinic registration card** if you are a returning patient

## What to Expect on Arrival

1. Proceed to the registration counter on the ground floor with your ID
2. Share your booking reference number (from SMS/app) or doctor's name
3. You will receive a token number and estimated wait time
4. Wait in the department waiting area indicated on your token
5. The doctor's assistant will call your token number when the doctor is ready
6. After your consultation, collect prescriptions and referrals from the counter before leaving

Allow 30-60 minutes for a standard consultation, including registration and waiting time. Specialist consultations (Cardiology, Orthopaedics) may take longer if initial diagnostic tests are ordered on the same visit.

## Cancellation Policy

If you need to cancel or reschedule, please do so at least 2 hours before your scheduled appointment. Late cancellations affect other patients who are waiting for slots.

To cancel: use the Apollo Health Clinic app (tap My Appointments > Cancel) or call reception at 080-33001100.

Rescheduling is free and can be done up to 2 hours before the appointment via the app or phone. No-shows for booked specialist slots may affect priority booking in future.

## Teleconsultation

Apollo Health Clinic offers video consultations for follow-up appointments and for patients who cannot visit in person.

**Available for:** General Medicine, Dermatology, Pulmonology (follow-up only)
**Not available for:** First-time specialist consultations requiring physical examination, all Radiology and Diagnostics services

To book a teleconsultation:
1. Open the Apollo Health Clinic app
2. Select a doctor and tap "Video Consult"
3. Choose an available time slot
4. Join the video call from the app at the scheduled time -- ensure your phone camera and microphone are working

You will receive a digital prescription after a teleconsultation. For investigations that require a physical sample (blood tests, X-rays), you will be asked to visit the clinic.

## Getting Test Results Online

Most lab results are available within 24-48 hours of the sample being collected. Imaging results (X-Ray, MRI) may take 24-72 hours depending on the test.

To access your reports:
1. Log in to the Apollo Health Clinic app
2. Tap My Reports
3. Reports are available as PDF downloads

If a result is not visible on the app within the expected timeframe, contact the Diagnostics department at the reception counter or call 080-33001100 ext. 2.

For any test result interpretation, please consult your treating doctor -- ClinicalIQ and clinic staff at reception cannot interpret test results.
