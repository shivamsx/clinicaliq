# Test Preparation Guide -- Apollo Health Clinic

This guide covers preparation instructions for common diagnostic tests at Apollo Health Clinic. Always follow any specific instructions given by your treating doctor -- those take priority over the general guidance here.

**Important:** ClinicalIQ cannot tell you which tests you need. That decision belongs to your doctor based on your clinical history and examination. This guide only explains how to prepare once a test has been ordered.

---

## Blood Tests

### Fasting Blood Tests
Tests that require fasting: Fasting Blood Sugar (FBS), Lipid Panel (cholesterol), Fasting Insulin, Uric Acid, Vitamin D, Iron Studies, Liver Function Test (LFT), Kidney Function Test (KFT), Full Blood Count / CBC.

**How to fast:**
- Fast for 8-12 hours before the test (no food or calorie-containing drinks)
- Plain water is permitted -- stay hydrated
- Continue your regular medications unless your doctor has specifically told you to stop them before the test (ask your doctor if unsure)
- Book your test for early morning so the fast falls during your sleeping hours

### Non-Fasting Blood Tests
Tests that do not require fasting: HbA1c (3-month blood sugar average), thyroid tests (TSH, T3, T4), complete blood count for infection/anaemia assessment.

For non-fasting tests, you may eat and drink normally unless your doctor specifies otherwise.

## ECG (Electrocardiogram)

**Preparation:**
- No fasting required
- Avoid applying body lotion or cream on your chest, arms, and legs on the day of the test -- electrodes need direct skin contact
- Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that is easy to remove (you will need to remove your top half)
- Remove jewellery from your wrists and ankles before the test
- Avoid vigorous exercise in the 30 minutes before the test

The ECG takes approximately 10-15 minutes. Results are available immediately and will be reviewed by a cardiologist.

---

## Echocardiogram (Echo)

**Preparation:**
- No fasting required for a standard transthoracic echo
- Wear comfortable clothing that allows access to the chest area
- Continue regular medications unless your cardiologist advises otherwise
- Avoid heavy meals immediately before the test

The echo takes approximately 30-45 minutes. A report is typically available within 24 hours.

---

## Chest X-Ray

**Preparation:**
- No fasting required
- Remove all jewellery and metal objects from the upper body
- You will be asked to remove clothing from the upper body and wear a clinic gown
- Inform the radiographer if you are pregnant or suspect you may be pregnant before any X-ray

The X-ray takes less than 5 minutes. Results are available within 24-48 hours.

---

## Pulmonary Function Test (PFT)

**Preparation:**
- Do not use your inhaler (bronchodilator) for 4-6 hours before the test, unless your pulmonologist specifically tells you to continue -- this allows the test to measure your true baseline lung function
- Avoid smoking for at least 4 hours before the test
- Avoid heavy meals for 2 hours before the test (a full stomach can affect breathing capacity)
- Wear loose, comfortable clothing that does not restrict your chest or abdomen
- Avoid vigorous exercise for 30 minutes before the test

The test involves breathing into a mouthpiece attached to a spirometer. A technician will coach you through a series of breathing manoeuvres. The test takes approximately 30-45 minutes.

---

## Skin Patch Test (Allergy Patch Test)

**Preparation:**
- Avoid applying any creams, lotions, or powders on your back in the 48 hours before the test
- Wear loose, comfortable clothing that allows easy access to your upper back
- Do not apply sunscreen on your back on the day of the test
- Inform your dermatologist if you are taking corticosteroids or antihistamines -- these may need to be paused before the test (do not stop medications without your doctor's advice)

The patch is applied to your back and left in place for 48 hours. You will need to return to the clinic for patch removal and a final reading at 72-96 hours. Avoid bathing or sweating heavily while the patch is in place.

---

## Bone Density Scan (DEXA Scan)

**Preparation:**
- No fasting required
- Avoid calcium supplements for 24 hours before the scan
- If you have had a barium X-ray, nuclear medicine test, or CT scan with contrast in the past 7 days, inform the Radiology department -- you may need to reschedule
- Remove metal objects, jewellery, and clothing with metal fasteners before the scan
- Wear comfortable, simple clothing

The scan takes approximately 20-30 minutes. Results are available within 24-48 hours.

---

## Urine Routine

**Preparation:**
- Collect the midstream portion of your first morning urine -- this is the most concentrated and gives the most accurate results
- Use the sterile container provided by the clinic (available at the Diagnostics counter)
- Do not collect urine directly from the toilet bowl
- Bring the sample to the Diagnostics counter within 1 hour of collection
- Avoid excessive fluid intake immediately before collection, as this dilutes the sample

---

## Vision and Eye Pressure Test (Ophthalmology)

**Preparation:**
- If you wear glasses or contact lenses, bring them to the appointment
- Remove contact lenses at least 30 minutes before the test
- Inform the ophthalmologist if you are taking any eye drops or systemic medications
- If dilation of the pupils is required (the doctor will advise), arrange for someone to drive you home -- your near vision will be blurred for 4-6 hours after dilation

---

## What to Bring for Any Investigation

- Doctor's referral slip (required for investigations ordered within the clinic; for self-referred walk-in tests, the reception counter can help you book)
- Previous reports for the same test (for comparison)
- Apollo Health Clinic registration card or registered mobile number
- Insurance card if claiming through health insurance
