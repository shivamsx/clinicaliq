# Patient Data Privacy Policy -- Apollo Health Clinic

**Effective date:** January 2024
**Governing law:** Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act)

---

## What Data Apollo Health Clinic Collects

When you register as a patient or use our services, we collect:

**Identity and contact information:** Full name, date of birth, gender, Aadhaar number (for identity verification), mobile number, email address, and home address.

**Medical information:** Consultation notes, diagnoses, prescribed medications, investigation results (lab reports, imaging reports), vital signs recorded during visits, and referrals issued by our doctors.

**Appointment and billing information:** Appointment history, services availed, payment records, and insurance claim details.

**Technical data:** If you use the Apollo Health Clinic app, we collect your device type and operating system to ensure app compatibility. We do not collect location data unless you explicitly grant permission for service-finding features.

---

## How Your Data is Used

Your data is used to:
- Provide you with medical care by your treating doctors
- Maintain your medical history for continuity of care across visits
- Process insurance claims on your behalf
- Send appointment reminders and health tips (you can opt out at any time)
- Comply with statutory reporting requirements (e.g., notifiable diseases as required by public health authorities)

We do not sell your data to third parties. We do not use your data for marketing by external organisations.

---

## Who Can Access Your Data

**Within Apollo Health Clinic:**
- Your treating doctor and their clinical team, for the purpose of providing you care
- The clinic's billing and insurance team, for processing payments and claims
- Clinic administration, for appointment management and compliance

**External parties:**
- Health insurance companies (TPAs) -- only the information required to process a specific claim, and only with your prior registration on the insurance panel
- Statutory authorities -- only when required by law (e.g., mandatory disease reporting, court orders)
- Diagnostic laboratories -- partner labs receive only the test request and your name; your full medical record is not shared

We require all external parties to comply with data protection obligations equivalent to those in the DPDP Act.

---

## How Long Records Are Kept

Medical records at Apollo Health Clinic are retained for a minimum of 7 years from the date of the last consultation, in line with the Medical Council of India guidelines and applicable state regulations.

After the retention period, records are securely destroyed.

Billing records are retained for 8 years as required by financial regulations.

---

## What ClinicalIQ Can and Cannot Access

**ClinicalIQ can help you with:**
- Booking, rescheduling, or cancelling appointments
- Identifying the right department for your concern
- Explaining what a test preparation involves
- Answering general questions about clinic services, hours, and policies

**ClinicalIQ cannot access:**
- Your personal medical records, test results, or consultation notes
- Your billing or insurance claim information
- Your contact details stored in our system

ClinicalIQ is a guidance assistant. It does not have access to any individually identifiable patient data. It can tell you about clinic policies and procedures, but it cannot look up information specific to your account.

## Your Rights Under the DPDP Act, 2023

As a data principal (the person whose data is held), you have the following rights:

**Right to access:** You may request a summary of the personal data we hold about you and the purposes for which it is being used.

**Right to correction:** You may request correction of any inaccurate or incomplete personal data -- for example, a misspelled name or outdated contact number.

**Right to erasure:** You may request deletion of your personal data in circumstances where we are no longer required to retain it by law. Note: medical records within the 7-year statutory retention period cannot be deleted on request.

**Right to grievance redressal:** If you believe your data is being processed in violation of the DPDP Act, you may raise a complaint with Apollo Health Clinic's Data Protection Officer. Unresolved complaints can be escalated to the Data Protection Board of India.

**How to exercise your rights:** Submit a written request to the clinic reception, addressed to the Data Protection Officer, with your registered name and mobile number for identity verification. We will respond within 30 days.

---

## Data Security

Patient records are stored on secured servers with access restricted to authorised staff. Physical records are stored in locked cabinets accessible only to clinical and administrative staff. All staff handling patient data are trained in confidentiality obligations.

In the event of a data breach that is likely to result in harm to you, Apollo Health Clinic will notify you and the Data Protection Board of India as required by the DPDP Act.
