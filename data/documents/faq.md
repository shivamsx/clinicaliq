# Frequently Asked Questions -- Apollo Health Clinic

---

**How do I book an appointment?**
You can book through the Apollo Health Clinic app (Android/iOS), by calling reception at 080-33001100, or by visiting the clinic in person. The app is the fastest option for most specialist departments. Walk-ins are also accepted subject to availability.

---

**Are walk-in patients accepted?**
Yes. Walk-in patients are accepted at all departments subject to doctor availability. However, booked patients are given priority. For specialist departments (Cardiology, Orthopaedics), walk-in wait times can be 1-3 hours during peak morning hours. Booking is strongly recommended for specialist consultations.

---

**What are the clinic's operating hours?**
Monday to Saturday: 8:00 am to 8:00 pm
Sunday: 9:00 am to 1:00 pm
The Emergency department operates 24 hours, 7 days a week.
Diagnostic services (blood tests, X-Ray) are available Monday to Saturday 7:30 am to 7:00 pm.

---

**How do I get my test results?**
Most lab results are available within 24-48 hours in the Apollo Health Clinic app under My Reports. Imaging results (X-Ray, MRI) may take 24-72 hours. If your result is not visible within the expected timeframe, contact the Diagnostics counter at ext. 2 on 080-33001100.

---

**Can ClinicalIQ give me a diagnosis?**
No. ClinicalIQ is a navigation and guidance assistant. It can help you find the right department, understand what a test preparation involves, answer questions about clinic services, and help you book appointments. It cannot assess your symptoms, diagnose conditions, or recommend treatments. For any medical assessment, please consult a doctor.

---

**How do I cancel or reschedule my appointment?**
Cancel or reschedule at least 2 hours before your appointment using the Apollo Health Clinic app (My Appointments > Cancel or Reschedule) or by calling 080-33001100. Rescheduling is free. Please avoid no-shows as they prevent other patients from accessing the slot.

---

**Does Apollo Health Clinic accept health insurance?**
Yes. Apollo Health Clinic is empanelled with major TPA (Third Party Administrator) networks. For a specific insurer, please visit the clinic or call reception -- empanelment status can change and the most current list is maintained at the billing counter. Bring your insurance card and any pre-authorisation letter issued by your insurer.

---

**Does Apollo Health Clinic accept Mediclaim policies?**
Apollo Health Clinic is empanelled with most major Mediclaim providers. Coverage depends on your specific policy terms, including the sum insured and the list of procedures covered under your plan. Please bring your Mediclaim card and a copy of your policy document on the day of your appointment. The billing desk will verify your coverage before the consultation. For a list of currently empanelled insurers, contact the clinic reception.

---

**Can I use my CGHS card at Apollo Health Clinic?**
Apollo Health Clinic accepts CGHS (Central Government Health Scheme) cards for eligible beneficiaries. CGHS-empanelled procedures and package rates apply. Please carry your CGHS card and beneficiary ID. For CGHS-specific queries about covered procedures or referral requirements, contact the clinic's insurance desk or the CGHS office in your city.

---

**Is there parking available at the clinic?**
Yes. Covered parking is available in the basement (entry from the side lane). Parking is available for up to 3 hours with a clinic visit. Two-wheelers can park in the designated area on the ground level. The parking is free for the first 2 hours for patients with a valid appointment receipt.

---

**Is teleconsultation available?**
Yes, for follow-up appointments in General Medicine, Dermatology, and Pulmonology. First-time consultations requiring a physical examination, and all Diagnostics services, require an in-person visit. Book a teleconsultation through the app by selecting "Video Consult" on the doctor's profile.

---

**What should I bring to my first appointment?**
Bring a photo ID (Aadhaar, PAN, or passport), any previous medical reports relevant to your concern, your current medications list, and your insurance card if applicable. For specialist consultations, bring any previous doctor's referral letter.

---

**How long will I wait for my reports?**
Blood test results: typically 24-48 hours.
ECG: available immediately after the test.
Chest X-Ray: 24-48 hours.
Echocardiogram: 24 hours.
MRI / CT Scan (if referred to empanelled centre): 48-72 hours.
Results appear in the app under My Reports as soon as they are ready.

---

**Can I ask ClinicalIQ whether I need a specific test?**
No. Deciding whether a test is clinically necessary is a medical decision that belongs to your treating doctor. ClinicalIQ can explain what a test is used for and how to prepare for it -- but it cannot tell you whether you personally need it.

---

**Which specialist should I see for back pain?**
Patients with back or joint pain typically see Orthopaedics. If the pain is accompanied by chest tightness or shortness of breath, please go to Emergency first. If you are unsure, start with General Medicine -- the doctor can assess and refer you to the appropriate specialist.

---

**Does the clinic handle emergencies?**
Yes. The Apollo Health Clinic Emergency department operates 24/7. For life-threatening situations (chest pain, difficulty breathing, stroke symptoms, severe injuries), go to Emergency directly -- do not wait at the OPD registration counter.

---

**How do I access my medical records?**
Your medical records are available through the app under My Reports (test results) and My Visits (consultation summaries, if available). For a complete printed copy of your records, submit a written request at the reception counter with your ID. Records are provided within 7 working days.

---

**Can someone else book an appointment on my behalf?**
Yes. A family member can book on your behalf using the app or by phone. Ensure they have your registered mobile number. If you are accompanying an elderly parent or child, book under the patient's name, not your own.

---

**What is ClinicalIQ and what can it not do?**
ClinicalIQ is Apollo Health Clinic's AI guidance assistant. It can help you book appointments, identify the right department, explain test preparation, and answer general questions about the clinic.

ClinicalIQ cannot: diagnose your condition, recommend medications or treatments, access your personal medical records or test results, process insurance claims, or make emergency assessments. For medical advice, please consult a doctor. For urgent situations, call 108 (emergency ambulance) or go to the Emergency department.

---

**Is my data safe with Apollo Health Clinic?**
Yes. Apollo Health Clinic complies with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023. Your medical records are accessible only to your treating doctors and authorised clinical staff. ClinicalIQ itself does not have access to your personal medical records. For details, see our Patient Data Privacy Policy.

---

**How do I raise a complaint about clinic services?**
Speak to the Patient Relations Officer at the reception desk, or email feedback@apollohealthclinic.in. We aim to respond within 3 working days. If your concern is not resolved, it can be escalated to the clinic management at management@apollohealthclinic.in.
