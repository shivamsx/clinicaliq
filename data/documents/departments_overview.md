# Departments Overview -- Apollo Health Clinic

This guide helps patients navigate to the right department for their concern. It is a navigation aid only -- it does not diagnose conditions or substitute for a doctor's assessment.

---

## When to Go to Emergency vs OPD

**Go to the Emergency department immediately if you experience:**
- Sudden chest pain or pressure, especially with breathlessness or sweating
- Sudden inability to speak, move a limb, or severe sudden headache (possible stroke signs)
- Difficulty breathing that is severe or rapidly worsening
- Uncontrolled bleeding
- Loss of consciousness or seizure
- Suspected poisoning or drug overdose
- Severe allergic reaction (throat swelling, difficulty swallowing)
- High fever in an infant under 3 months

**Come to OPD (Outpatient Department) for:**
- Symptoms that have developed over several days or weeks
- Follow-up consultations
- Routine health checkups and preventive screenings
- Chronic condition management

When in doubt, go to Emergency -- the triage team will redirect you to OPD if appropriate.

## General Medicine

**What this department handles:**
Fever, cough, cold, fatigue, body aches, headaches, routine health checkups, and general health concerns that do not clearly belong to a specialist. General Medicine is the right first stop for most new health concerns.

**Typical patient queries for General Medicine:**
- Patients with fever lasting more than 3 days typically see General Medicine
- Patients with persistent fatigue or unexplained weight loss typically see General Medicine for an initial workup
- Patients seeking annual health checkups typically start with General Medicine
- Children aged 16 and above with general concerns typically see General Medicine (under 16, see Paediatrics)

If the General Medicine doctor determines a specialist is needed, they will issue a referral.

---

## Cardiology

**What this department handles:**
Heart health concerns including chest pain, palpitations, breathlessness on exertion, high blood pressure management, and follow-up for known heart conditions.

**Typical patient queries for Cardiology:**
- Patients with chest discomfort or palpitations typically see Cardiology
- Patients with blood pressure concerns not controlled by primary care typically see Cardiology
- Patients with a family history of heart disease seeking a cardiac screening typically see Cardiology
- Patients referred after an abnormal ECG typically see Cardiology

**Important:** Sudden, severe chest pain or pain with breathlessness and sweating should go to Emergency, not OPD.

Common investigations ordered by Cardiology: ECG, Echocardiogram, Lipid Panel, 24-hour BP monitoring.

---

## Paediatrics

**What this department handles:**
Health concerns for children under 16 years of age. This includes routine checkups (growth monitoring, vaccinations), fever, cough, digestive issues, developmental assessments, and general child health.

**Typical patient queries for Paediatrics:**
- Parents bringing children under 16 for any health concern
- Vaccination schedules and developmental milestones
- Recurrent cough, cold, or ear infections in children
- Digestive issues (diarrhoea, vomiting, poor appetite) in children

Patients aged 16 and above should consult General Medicine or the relevant specialist, not Paediatrics.

---

## Orthopaedics

**What this department handles:**
Bones, joints, muscles, and sports injuries. This includes joint pain (knee, hip, shoulder), back pain, fractures, sprains, and post-surgical rehabilitation.

**Typical patient queries for Orthopaedics:**
- Patients with persistent joint pain or swelling typically see Orthopaedics
- Patients with a recent injury (fall, sports impact) affecting movement typically see Orthopaedics
- Patients with lower back pain that has not improved with rest typically see Orthopaedics
- Patients seeking post-fracture follow-up typically see Orthopaedics

Common investigations ordered by Orthopaedics: X-Ray, MRI, Bone Density Scan.

---

## Dermatology

**What this department handles:**
Skin, hair, and nail conditions. This includes acne, eczema, psoriasis, fungal infections, hair loss, rashes, moles, and cosmetic skin concerns.

**Typical patient queries for Dermatology:**
- Patients with a persistent skin rash or new skin lesion typically see Dermatology
- Patients experiencing unexplained hair loss typically see Dermatology
- Patients with a nail infection or nail abnormality typically see Dermatology
- Patients with acne not responding to over-the-counter treatments typically see Dermatology

---

## Ophthalmology

**What this department handles:**
Eye health including vision problems, eye infections, glaucoma screening, diabetic eye checkups, and dry eye syndrome.

**Typical patient queries for Ophthalmology:**
- Patients with blurred or declining vision typically see Ophthalmology
- Patients with eye redness, discharge, or pain typically see Ophthalmology
- Patients with diabetes seeking an annual eye checkup typically see Ophthalmology
- Patients with a family history of glaucoma seeking a pressure check typically see Ophthalmology

Common investigations ordered by Ophthalmology: Vision test, intraocular pressure measurement, fundus examination.

---

## ENT (Ear, Nose, and Throat)

**What this department handles:**
Conditions affecting the ear (hearing loss, ear pain, infections), nose (blocked nose, sinusitis, nosebleeds), and throat (sore throat, voice changes, difficulty swallowing).

**Typical patient queries for ENT:**
- Patients with persistent ear pain or hearing difficulty typically see ENT
- Patients with recurrent sinusitis or blocked nasal passages typically see ENT
- Patients with a persistent sore throat or voice hoarseness lasting more than 2 weeks typically see ENT
- Patients with snoring or sleep-related breathing concerns may see ENT

Common investigations ordered by ENT: Audiometry (hearing test), nasal endoscopy.

---

## Pulmonology

**What this department handles:**
Lung and respiratory conditions including asthma, COPD, chronic cough, breathlessness, and sleep apnoea.

**Typical patient queries for Pulmonology:**
- Patients with persistent cough lasting more than 3 weeks typically see Pulmonology or General Medicine first
- Patients with known asthma seeking specialist follow-up typically see Pulmonology
- Patients with breathlessness during daily activities (not sudden or severe) typically see Pulmonology
- Patients with suspected sleep apnoea (heavy snoring, daytime sleepiness) typically see Pulmonology

Common investigations ordered by Pulmonology: Pulmonary Function Test (PFT), Chest X-Ray, sleep study.
